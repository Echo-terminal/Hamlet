About «Hamlet»

"The Tragic Story of Hamlet, Prince of Denmark," or shortly "Hamlet," is a tragedy by William Shakespeare in five acts, one of his most famous plays and among the most renowned works in world drama. It was written between 1599 and 1601. It's Shakespeare's longest play, containing 4,042 lines and 29,551 words. The play is set in Denmark, where Prince Hamlet seeks revenge on his uncle Claudius for murdering his father to seize the throne and marry Hamlet's mother. This tragedy is part of the World Library (a list of the most significant works in world literature by the Norwegian Book Club, based on a survey of 100 critics and writers from various countries). 'Hamlet' was one of Shakespeare's most popular works during his lifetime and remains so to this day. It's considered one of the most adapted plots for the screen after the fairy tale 'Cinderella.'
